Pair Programming with the Browser-based Cube

  • Get the browser-based interface for the L3D cube set up locally on your computer.
  • Find a partner and try to make some of these extensions to the existing interface (or develop your own):
    1. Make the 'LEDs' fade as they move back through the slices of the cube.
    2. Make an 'LED' disappear after it reaches the back slice.
    3. Make it so pressing a letter creates the letter on the front slice of the cube and sends it towards the back.
    4. Find a way to save the state of your browser-based cube so you can access it later.
    5. Extend the code so that you can reproduce the above behavior for the other directions (top to bottom and left to right).

For next week:

  1. Mess around with the extensions to the browser-based L3D interface. See if you can get them working, and let us know if you run into challenges.
  2. Explore the materials on Discourse about digital art-,art-making, and LED art. Come in next week with some ideas of digital art project(s) you'd like to make and some sense of why they're exciting to you.
  3. Share your 2+2 (you can read more on sharing your 2+2's here)
  4. A reminder: Each week, we'd love your feedback on the session!
