L3D updates

Tessel.io

  1. npm install -g tessel and, after connecting your Tessel, tessel repl should let you interactively control it.
  2. Try controlling the four LEDs on the Tessel board (↪ Blinky)
  3. Display 0–15 on the Tessel LEDs as a binary counter (↪ "Numbers & bases")
  4. Blink a plugged-in LED by connecting one leg to GND and another to a GPIO pin which you set HIGH and LOW (↪ Arduino)
  5. Turn the LED on/off by pressing a button (↪ "Adding buttons to Tessel")
  6. Use one of the provided sensors and log its reading at regular intervals (↪ LED as light sensor)
  7. Blink the LEDs in (3) or (4) at a rate proportional to the light reading from (6)
  8. Log out the readings from one of the modules that interest you in the terminal
