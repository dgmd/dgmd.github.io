Project Specifications

Structuring a useful, usable, reflective process for sketching out and iterating on your design is inevitably a very personal matter. But that doesn't mean that all approaches are created equal. We thought it would be useful to share a smattering of firsthand thoughts and experiences from expert designers on their process of sketching and specifying. If there are others you've run across which you think folks would benefit from knowing about, please let us know. In no particular order:
  • James Buckhouse's "Story Map"
  • Jason Fried's "Sketching with a Sharpie"
  • Joel Spolsky's "Painless Functional Specifications" series
  • Francis Cortez's "Personal Design Sprint #1", #2, and #3
  • Alexander Cowan's "Your Best Agile User Story"
  • A few, guiding questions from DGMD E-15

Some good questions

  1. What is the project's mission?
  2. Who is its audience, and what do they value?
  3. How will you know if it is successful?
  4. What is your plan for going about it?

Workshopping your projects

  1. Find a partner…
  2. Spend 5-10 minutes describing your project—
    1. What is the project's mission?
    2. Who is its audience, and what do they value?
    3. How will you know if it is successful?
    4. What is your plan for going about it?
    5. What's your first sketch of the storyboard?
  3. Post your notes, sketches, etc. to your journal.
