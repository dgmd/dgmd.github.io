One of the most common mistakes in programs like this is not asking for enough help and/or not asking for help enough. Small, frequent course corrections (whether on choosing a project, debugging an exercise, or just wrapping your head around a new concept) are more fun and more likely to succeed. So, please find a way to reach out!

If your question is technical—i.e. if we would benefit from seeing your code (and it's failure or success) please file an Issue. You can read more in the GitHub section of Tools & Materials.

If you're comfortable sharing your question with others, consider posting a question to our forum, and if your question is a general, technical question (i.e. doesn't intimately depend on a particular project we've provided), you should also consider (searching for an answer to, then) posting your question to StackOverflow.

For all other types of questions, feel free to do one of the following:

  • Email us at dgmde15@gmail.com
  • Email just one of us: mikhak@fas.harvard.edu (Bakhtiar), shaunalynn@thesprouts.org (Shaunalynn), rfboyce@gmail.com (Rachel), alec@thesprouts.org (Alec),
  • Sign up for and post a question on our Discourse forum
  • Come to one of our Saturday or Sunday studio sessions, 9AM–1PM at Clover in Harvard Square
